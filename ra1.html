ROBO RUSH

Event Description

Participants must operate a manually controlled robot, either wired or wireless, to navigate a track filled with obstacles and sharp turns in the shortest possible time.

RULES AND REGULATIONS

  • The competition is open to all participants (individuals or team).
  • Each team can have a maximum of 2-3 members.
  • Only one robot per team is allowed.
  • Participants must report 30 minutes before the event starts.
  • Any team not ready at the time of their match will be disqualified.
  • No exchange of bots and controllers are allowed.
  • Participants should keep spare batteries, otherwise it may lead to disqualification if bot is not ready or stops in between the race.
  • 5-second penalty for touching the boundary line.
  • 10-second penalty for manually touching the robot during the race.
  • Disqualification if the team violates any safety rules.
  • The team that completes the track with least time wins.
  • In case of a tie, a rematch will be held.
  • If a robot gets stuck for more than 15 seconds, it must restart from the last checkpoint.
  • The judges decision is final and binding.

Robot Specifications

  • The maximum weight allowed is 5kg.
  • The robot can be wired or wireless. If wired, the wire length should be at least 3 meters.
  • The robot must be powered by onboard batteries (maximum 12V allowed).
  • The robot must not contain hazardous, fire-causing, or explosive materials.

Race Track Rules

  • The track will have turns, inclines, speed breakers, and obstacles.
  • The robot must stay within the track boundaries; moving out results in penalties.
  • The race will be time-based ‚Äì the fastest robot to complete the track wins.
  • There will be checkpoint zones where the robot must cross properly.
